# Mindspan — Design Context

## Users

**Primary:** Caregivers and family members (most often a spouse, adult child, or close family member) of someone experiencing memory loss, cognitive change, Alzheimer's, or dementia. They are exhausted, frustrated by the healthcare system's long wait times, and quietly terrified. Many arrive after months or years of noticing changes.

**Secondary:** Patients themselves — typically older adults noticing changes in memory, attention, or cognition. Often brought in by a caregiver or urged by their PCP.

**Referral partners:** Primary care physicians who want a fast, trusted specialist partner for cognitive concerns.

**Emotional context when they arrive:** scared, overwhelmed, guilty, skeptical after past bad experiences with healthcare. They need calm reassurance and a clear next step — not a sales pitch.

## Brand Personality

Three words: **warm, clinical, grounded.**

Voice: like a brilliant neurologist friend. Direct, kind, specific, never hedging. Removes helplessness, backs it up with clinical reality.

Never: pitch-deck, wellness-brand, salesy, overly enthusiastic, cold, corporate.

**Hard rules from the Mindspan Content & PR Posture Guide:**
- Never say "more study is required"
- Never say "consult your doctor" (we ARE the doctors)
- Never "results may vary"
- Never em dashes (commas instead)
- Never sound like a wellness brand
- Always claim clinical identity: "We are neurologists. We bill insurance. In clinic or virtual."

## Aesthetic Direction

**Visual style:** Editorial magazine meets medical trust. Warm neutral backgrounds, confident serif headlines, restrained pale blue accents. Think New Yorker article meets premium clinic brochure. Calm, considered, spacious, never loud.

**Theme:** Light, with dark brand-green sections for emphasis. Never dark default.

**Type pairing:**
- Display/headlines: **PT Serif** (warm editorial serif)
- Accent/italic: **Bitter** (serif italic for flair)
- Body/UI: **Inter** (neutral sans for readability)
- All loaded via next/font/google

**Color palette (locked):**
- Brand green: `#083630` (primary, deep forest)
- Brand green light: `#1f514a`
- Brand orange: `#fb4d17` (accent, CTAs)
- Cream: `#efeeeb` (page bg)
- Sand: `#dad6c9` (alt section bg)
- Sky: `#bdd8f5` (blue accent — used as icon tiles, card backgrounds, banner, NEVER as full section backgrounds)
- Sky soft: `#d9e6f3` (softer tint)

**Established patterns:**
- Section rhythm: eyebrow (uppercase Inter 600, orange) → h2 (PT Serif, fluid clamp) → lead paragraph (62ch max) → content
- Cards: 1.5rem radius, subtle 1px borders at rgba(8,54,48,0.08), 3D button lift with layered shadows
- Icons: 48×48 pale blue rounded-square tiles with dark green Lucide glyphs at strokeWidth 1.8, rounded caps and joins
- Animation: framer-motion scroll-triggered fade-up (24px, 0.7s, cubic-bezier(0.22, 0.61, 0.36, 1)), stagger containers with 80ms child delay, once: true
- Buttons: solid 3D (inset top highlight + layered drop shadow), lift on hover, press on active
- NO em dashes anywhere. Only commas.
- NO pitch-deck framing ("Four Proof Points", "What makes us different")
- NO gradient text, NO side-stripe borders, NO glassmorphism

## Design Principles

1. **Calm over loud.** The audience is anxious. Every interaction should lower the heart rate, not raise it.
2. **Editorial restraint.** Less text, more whitespace. Every section earns its place.
3. **Specific over generic.** Name things (Cognitive Digital Twin, Care Orchestration Engine, Mindy). Concrete beats aspirational.
4. **Warm clinical.** Serif editorial feel + clear clinical identity ("we bill insurance, we're neurologists"). Neither soft wellness nor cold corporate.
5. **Blue accents only.** Pale blue is a minor accent used in icon tiles, the Core Protocol card, step numbers, and the banner. Never as a full section background.
